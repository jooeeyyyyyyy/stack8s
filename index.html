How It Works

From idea to production
in five steps

Step 1 - Core Zone

Set up your environment (cluster) and select your Core Zone ‚Äì the foundational location where your control plane (master nodes) will reside.

This is the brain of your compute cluster, responsible for managing workloads, networking, and orchestration.

üí° Tip: You can add more zones and nodes later

Step 2 - Projects

Projects are your first layer of multi-tenancy, designed to keep environments clean, secure, and organized. Each project is network-isolated from the others and acts as a container for related Stacks.

üì¶ Use projects to separate teams, clients, or environments while maintaining full isolation and control.

Step 3 - Stacks

Stacks (mapped to Kubernetes namespaces) are your second layer of multi-tenancy. They act as virtual clusters within your project, used to organize and isolate compute resources, workloads, and configurations.

üß© Each Stack gives you fine-grained control over deployments‚Äîideal for separating teams, applications, or environments within the same project.

Step 4 - Add Components

Explore the Component Marketplace to supercharge your stack. Choose from 100+ pre-packaged components‚Äîincluding Ollama, ChromaDB, Kafka, TensorFlow, and RStudio‚Äîor bring your own custom container.

üîß Whether you're building ML pipelines, deploying agents, or spinning up dev tools, plug-and-play components make setup instant and scalable.

Step 5 - Scale Instantly

Horizontal scaling with GPUs, CPUs, and storage has never been simpler. Stack8s auto-scales your infrastructure based on demand, ensuring optimal performance and cost efficiency.
